# Insufficient evidence (reference)

Treat evidence gaps seriously: **surface them to the manager in one consolidated ask** before writing final narratives where possible; **do not** silently ignore gaps. If the manager cannot supply more input, **proceed** with explicit **Low** or **provisional** confidence — **never** block the entire review waiting for perfect evidence.

## Step 1 — Identify gaps during calibration (Step 2 of workflow)

After reading all inputs, identify every competency where confidence is Low. For each gap, determine:

- What specific evidence is missing
- What type of input would resolve it (see [reference-role-evidence.md](reference-role-evidence.md))

## Step 2 — Pause and ask the manager before proceeding

Do not move to writing assessments. Instead, surface all gaps to the manager in a single consolidated ask:

```
Before I write the assessments, I've identified some evidence gaps that would affect rating confidence.
Can you provide any of the following?

For [Engineer Name]:
- [Competency]: I'm missing [specific evidence type]. For example: a summary of your 1:1 notes,
  any product metrics tied to their work, or peer feedback on [specific behaviour].
- [Competency]: [same pattern]

You can paste notes directly — even rough bullet points are useful. If you don't have anything
for a given item, just say so and I'll flag it as low-confidence in the assessment.
```

Note: peer feedback should already have been collected in the earlier step. If it was skipped
or the manager indicated they had none, name peer feedback explicitly here for any competency
where confidence is Low — particularly Leadership and Impact.

Key behaviours:

- **Consolidate all gaps into one ask** — do not ask piecemeal across multiple messages
- **Be specific** — name the competency, the missing evidence type, and give a concrete example of what would help
- **Make it easy to respond** — explicitly tell the manager that rough notes, bullet points, or even verbal summaries are acceptable inputs
- **Accept partial responses** — if the manager can't fill a gap, proceed with Low confidence flagged; never block the entire review waiting for perfect evidence

## Step 3 — Incorporate additional evidence

Once the manager responds (or confirms they have nothing more to add):

- Integrate any new inputs into your calibration table
- Update confidence ratings accordingly
- Proceed to writing assessments

## Step 4 — Flag residual gaps in the output

For any competency where evidence remains insufficient after the evidence-gathering step:

- Assign a provisional rating with explicit Low confidence notation
- State clearly what evidence would confirm or change the rating
- Do not present Low-confidence ratings with the same authority as well-evidenced ones

Example notation:

```
Leadership
Rating: PRACTITIONER (provisional — low confidence)
Evidence gap: No peer feedback available and limited direct observation of mentoring behaviour.
This rating is based solely on self-assessment examples. Recommend validating with peer input
or direct observation in the next quarter.
```

Never inflate confidence to produce a cleaner output. A clearly flagged provisional rating is
more valuable than a fabricated one.
